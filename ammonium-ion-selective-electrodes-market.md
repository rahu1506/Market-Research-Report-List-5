<p><p>Ammonium ion selective electrodes are sensors used to detect and measure the concentration of ammonium ions in a solution. There are two main types of electrodes used in the market: liquid membrane and PVC membrane electrodes. Liquid membrane electrodes use a thin liquid layer to separate the sample from an internal electrolyte solution, while PVC membrane electrodes use a polyvinyl chloride membrane to selectively bind to ammonium ions. Both types of electrodes offer high sensitivity and specificity in detecting ammonium ions.</p></p>
