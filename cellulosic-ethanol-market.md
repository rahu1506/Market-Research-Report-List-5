<p><p>The cellulosic ethanol market utilizes various feedstocks, including corn stover, sugarcane straw, and bagasse, among others. Corn stover, the leftover plant material after corn harvest, is abundant and cost-effective. Sugarcane straw, a byproduct of sugar production, offers high sugar content for fermentation. Bagasse, the fibrous residue from crushed sugarcane, also serves as a valuable feedstock due to its cellulose-rich composition. Other emerging sources include agricultural residues and dedicated energy crops, contributing to sustainable biofuel production and reducing reliance on fossil fuels.</p></p>
