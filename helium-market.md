<p><p>The helium market consists of two primary types: liquid helium and gaseous helium. Liquid helium is used extensively in cryogenics, particularly for cooling superconducting magnets in MRI machines and particle accelerators. It is valued for its low boiling point and efficiency in maintaining low temperatures. Gaseous helium, on the other hand, is employed in various applications, including industrial processes, home balloons, and as a protective inert gas in welding. Both segments play vital roles in advancing technology and scientific research.</p></p>
