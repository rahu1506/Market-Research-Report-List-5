<p><p>Patient Controlled Analgesia (PCA) pumps are devices used in hospitals to administer pain medication to patients. There are two main types of PCA pumps: electronic and mechanical. </p><p>Electronic PCA pumps use advanced technology to deliver precise doses of pain medication at the push of a button by the patient. These pumps are programmable and allow healthcare providers to closely monitor the dosage and frequency of medication.</p><p>On the other hand, mechanical PCA pumps are simpler in design and rely on manual adjustments by the patient to control the delivery of pain medication. These pumps are typically used in settings where electronic devices are not available or feasible.</p></p>
