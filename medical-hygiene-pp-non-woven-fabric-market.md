<p><ul><li>Drylaid</li><li>Airlaid</li><li>Wetlaid</li><li>Spunlaid</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Medical hygiene PP non woven fabric is primarily produced through four main techniques: Drylaid, Airlaid, Wetlaid, and Spunlaid. Drylaid involves laying fibers onto a moving belt without any water. Airlaid utilizes air to form a web of fibers. Wetlaid involves combining fibers with water and a chemical solution to form a web. Spunlaid uses heat to melt and extrude fibers onto a conveyor belt. Each technique has its own advantages and is used in various applications within the medical hygiene industry.</p></p>
