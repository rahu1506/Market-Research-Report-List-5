<p><p>The enameled wire market comprises three primary types: copper, aluminum, and copper-clad aluminum (CCA). Copper enameled wire is known for its excellent conductivity and is widely used in motors and transformers. Aluminum enameled wire offers a lighter alternative with good conductivity but is often less preferred for high-performance applications. Copper-clad aluminum combines the benefits of both materials, featuring a thin layer of copper over aluminum, providing a balance between weight, cost, and conductivity, making it versatile for various applications.</p></p>
