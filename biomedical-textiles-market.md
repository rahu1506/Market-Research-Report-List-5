<p><p>Biomedical textiles can be categorized into three main types based on their structure and manufacturing process: non-woven textiles, woven textiles, and knitted textiles. Non-woven textiles are made by bonding or interlocking fibers together without weaving, offering durability and breathability. Woven textiles are created by weaving yarns together to form a tight, stable structure, providing strength and shape retention. Knitted textiles are constructed by looping yarns together, offering flexibility and stretchability. Each type of biomedical textile plays a unique role in various medical applications.</p></p>
