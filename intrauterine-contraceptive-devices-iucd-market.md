<p><p>Intrauterine Contraceptive Devices (IUCD) come in various types based on their composition and mechanism of action. Non-hormonal IUCDs work by creating a hostile environment for sperm, preventing fertilization. Hormonal IUCDs release hormones that thicken cervical mucus, making it difficult for sperm to reach the egg. Inert IUCDs are made of materials like copper or plastic that do not have any hormonal effects. Each type of IUCD offers women different options for birth control based on their preferences and needs.</p></p>
